The patient underwent primary low transverse cesarean section on 2014-05-19 at approximately 28 weeks gestation . This was performed in this location in case any acute cardiac decompensation occurred during the time of cesarean section . She had followup with her primary cardiologist in Shaugnessy-Kaplan Rehab Hospital scheduled for 1 week postoperatively . She was evaluated upon presentation by the Maternal Fetal Medicine attending on call as well as by Cardiology . She has known dilated cardiomyopathy with a baseline ejection fraction of approximately 25% . The cesarean section was performed on the West Campus in the Cardiothoracic Surgical Suite . The patient was given antenatal corticosteroids for fetal lung maturity . She was found to have symptoms of congestive heart failure as well as decreased fetal movement . Symptoms of congestive heart failure gradually abated over the next 48 to 72 hours . The patient was transferred to the West Campus on postoperative day #2 in stable condition . The patient went to the cardiac Intensive Care Unit on the West Campus postoperatively . She was to followup with Maternal Fetal Medicine in 5 to 7 days . She also took albuterol on a p.r.n. Cardiothoracic Surgery was on standby during the surgery . She was discharged to home on postoperative day #4 . Fetal monitoring initially on admission was appropriate for gestational age . The patient was carefully diuresed over the next 48 hours .
